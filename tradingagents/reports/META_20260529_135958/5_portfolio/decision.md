**Investment Thesis**: This Underweight decision reflects a hard-fought synthesis where both the bull and bear cases carry genuine weight, but the bears win on the balance of near-term risk/reward.

The bull case is anchored in exceptional fundamentals that are not in dispute: Q1 2026 delivered 33% revenue growth ($56.3B), 40% operating margins, $26.8B in net income, and a PEG ratio of 0.92—statistically cheap for this margin structure. The MACD printed a textbook bullish crossover in late May with the histogram accelerating from +1.54 to +2.73, RSI sits at a healthy 56.9 with room to run, and the AI narrative has structural catalysts in the CoreWeave $21B commitment, Broadcom custom-chip confirmation, and Anthropic's soaring valuation. These positives are why this is not a Sell—they prevent capitulation and justify maintaining substantial core exposure.

However, three bear arguments carry decisive weight for the near term. First, the technical picture is not bullish: the 200 SMA is declining at ~$667 and has not been reclaimed, the death cross remains in effect, price sits above the upper Bollinger band—a condition the technical report itself flags as overextended with medium confidence, carrying a 70% statistical mean-reversion tendency—and the May 28 volume stall (15M shares vs. 23M on the breakout day, flat close at $635.29) signals buyer exhaustion rather than healthy consolidation. The MACD crossover, while real, is occurring from deeply negative levels after a V-shaped 21% recovery, mirroring the late-March setup that rallied to $688 before reversing violently at the 200 SMA. The conservative analyst is correct: the conditions are not identical (RSI was 70 then vs. 57 now), but the key structural parallel—a declining 200 SMA acting as a lid—remains unresolved.

Second, the CapEx math is sobering. Meta's annualized $69.7B in capital expenditure generates only $46.1B in free cash flow—a 0.66x return that has barely ticked to 0.70x over two quarters. Debt has doubled from $37B to $87B in two years, and the $218B PPE base is loaded with AI infrastructure whose monetization path remains unproven at scale. The bull's comparison to Amazon's 2015 fulfillment-center buildout is aspirational but unconfirmed; Amazon's retail monetization was proven, Meta's AI revenue streams (Cloud, enhanced ad targeting, Reels) are still emerging. The PEG of 0.92 is not a discount waiting to be corrected—it is the market pricing in exactly this uncertainty.

Third, two underappreciated tail risks cannot be dismissed as noise. The Pentagon ad-data story—where the U.S. military publicly stated ad data was used to target troops—is a direct threat to the 98% of revenue derived from advertising. The bull argues the stock rallied 6% since the story broke, but markets routinely underprice low-probability, high-impact regulatory events until legislation crystallizes. The CoreWeave counterparty exposure—$21B committed to a company losing $740M per quarter—introduces financial contagion risk if the AI infrastructure bubble deflates. These are not balance-sheet events today, but they are asymmetric risks that warrant reduced exposure, not full conviction.

The Neutral analyst's 20% trim recommendation is the most calibrated execution point: it reduces dollar risk on the remaining position while retaining 80% exposure to the 80% combined probability of neutral ($598–$667 range) or bullish (breakout above $667) outcomes. The Aggressive analyst's 15% trim gives too little weight to the technical resistance and tail risks. The Conservative analyst's 25% trim overweights the 20% bearish scenario and creates excessive opportunity cost if the 200 SMA breaks. Twenty percent is the point where capital preservation and upside participation intersect, consistent with the Research Manager's directive to reduce exposure toward the lower end of the allocation range without exiting a high-quality business.
