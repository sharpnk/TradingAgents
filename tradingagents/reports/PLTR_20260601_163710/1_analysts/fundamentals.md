## 6. Key Strengths & Growth Drivers

1. **AIP Platform Momentum:** Palantir's Artificial Intelligence Platform is driving massive commercial adoption. The company's "boot camp" sales strategy converts trials into long-term contracts.
2. **Government Contracts:** Deep relationships with US and allied defense/intelligence agencies provide stable, long-term revenue.
3. **Operating Leverage:** Revenue grew 56% in FY2025 while operating expenses grew slower, leading to margin expansion.
4. **Capital-Light Model:** 82%+ gross margins and minimal CapEx mean most revenue eventually flows to FCF.
5. **Cash-Rich:** $8B in cash provides strategic flexibility for M&A or investments.

## 7. Key Risks & Concerns

1. **Extreme Valuation:** 180x P/E leaves no room for error. Any slowdown in growth could trigger severe multiple compression.
2. **Stock-Based Compensation Dilution:** $684M in SBC (FY2025) and $202M in Q1 2026 alone. Shares outstanding grew from ~2.09B (2022) to ~2.57B (Q1 2026), a 23% increase in 4 years.
3. **Government Dependency Risk:** A significant portion of revenue comes from government contracts, which can be lumpy and subject to budget cycles.
4. **High Expectations:** The stock has priced in perfection. Q1 2026 revenue of $1.63B implies ~$6.5B+ annualized, which needs to sustain.
5. **Competition:** Microsoft, Snowflake, Databricks, and other AI/analytics platforms compete for similar workloads.
6. **Negative Retained Earnings:** Despite profitability, the company still has -$2.69B in retained earnings, indicating past losses haven't been fully recovered.
