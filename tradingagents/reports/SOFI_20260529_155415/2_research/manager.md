**Recommendation**: Underweight

**Rationale**: This was a hard-fought debate with compelling data on both sides, but after weighing the arguments, the bear case carries slightly more weight for the near term.

The bull made a genuinely strong fundamental case: 42.5% YoY revenue growth, 134% net income acceleration, debt slashed 66% from $5.6B to $1.9B, five consecutive earnings beats, SBC declining as a percentage of revenue, and a CEO putting his own capital to work at $15-$17. That is real operational momentum, and it's why a Sell rating would be an overreaction. The bull also correctly notes that negative operating cash flow is largely a function of record loan originations that will generate net interest income over the next 3-5 years—NII was $693M in Q1, up 39% YoY.

But the bear landed the more decisive blows where it counts for near-term positioning. First, the technical picture remains bearish at the structural level: the 50-day SMA is still declining, the 200-day SMA is $23.24 and falling—37% above the current price—and the stock closed above the upper Bollinger Band, which statistically favors mean reversion. The MACD crossover and RSI recovery are real but they confirm a move that already happened; they don't predict the next leg. Second, the $18-$19 zone is a genuine resistance graveyard where April dip-buyers will be looking to break even and sell—the bull's own "first target" is also the bear's supply zone. Third, the stablecoin launch, while strategically interesting, has generated zero revenue and prices in regulatory outcomes (Clarity Act) that haven't materialized. Fourth, a debt-to-equity ratio of 17.7x remains elevated even after the deleveraging progress, and negative $2.31B in quarterly operating cash flow creates genuine dependency on favorable capital markets. Fifth, QoQ revenue growth decelerating from 12.5% to 7.3% is a yellow flag that the bear rightly flagged—even if YoY remains impressive.

The bull needs everything to continue going right to reach the 200-day SMA at $23 (35% upside). The bear only needs one thing—a credit hiccup, a regulatory delay, or a growth miss—to see a retest of $12-$15 (12-30% downside). That asymmetry tilts the risk/reward against aggressive buying at current levels. However, the strength of the bull's fundamental data and the genuine momentum signals prevent a Sell rating. The right stance is to tread carefully: acknowledge the recovery narrative but respect the technical and balance-sheet headwinds that make this rally vulnerable.

**Strategic Actions**: 1. **Reduce position size if overweight**: If SOFI represents an outsized position, trim into strength toward the $18-$19 resistance zone where April's trapped buyers are likely to sell. Use this zone to right-size exposure.

2. **No new buying until technical confirmation**: Wait for the 50-day SMA to flatten and turn upward, or for a successful close above $19 on sustained volume (50M+ shares across multiple sessions, not just a Friday headline spike). A confirmed hold above the 50-day SMA for two consecutive weeks would be a more constructive entry signal.

3. **Set a hard stop on any existing position**: Place a stop-loss at or slightly below the March low of $15.15. A break below that level would invalidate the "higher low" thesis and open the door to $12. This limits downside to approximately 11% from current levels while preserving upside exposure if the recovery continues.

4. **Monitor the Q2 2026 earnings report for three specific data points**: (a) whether QoQ revenue growth stabilizes or continues decelerating from 7.3%, (b) operating cash flow trajectory—any improvement in the quarterly burn rate, and (c) tangible revenue or member-adoption metrics from SoFiUSD. A beat-and-raise quarter with improving cash flow would materially strengthen the bull case and justify re-rating upward.

5. **If short, cover into the $18-$19 zone rather than pressing**: The bull's fundamental data (134% net income growth, CEO buying, debt reduction) creates genuine short-squeeze risk. A catalyst-driven breakout above $19 could force a rapid re-rating toward the 200-day SMA. Shorts should take profits at resistance, not fight the tape if volume confirms a breakout.

6. **Revisit the rating on a confirmed break above $20 on volume**: Such a move would clear the April consolidation graveyard, likely flatten the 50-day SMA, and shift the technical picture from bear-market rally to genuine trend reversal. At that point, an upgrade to Overweight would be warranted.
