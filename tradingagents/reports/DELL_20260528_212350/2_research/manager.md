### Evaluation of the Debate

The bull weaves a compelling narrative around AI-driven earnings acceleration, record free cash flow, and a forward P/E that appears cheap. - Forward EPS estimate of $17.25 yields a PEG ratio near 1.3, suggesting the stock is not expensive *if* the growth materialises.
- Free cash flow of $8.55 billion provides ample flexibility for buybacks and debt service.

**Key judgment:**
The bull argues that the stock’s 176% rally is justified by earnings acceleration, but the data shows the multiple on *realised* earnings has expanded dramatically. That makes the current price a bet on flawless execution over the next several quarters—a bet with very little margin of safety. The bear’s dissection of the balance sheet and technicals reveals a high probability of a sharp mean-reversion event. The asymmetry is clear: upside to $350 is about 15%, while a reversion to even the 20‑day SMA ($244) would be a 20% loss. That negative risk/reward, combined with frothy retail sentiment and institutional quiet, warrants a cautious posture.
