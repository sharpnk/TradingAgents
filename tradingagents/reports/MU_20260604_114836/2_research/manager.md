The Broadcom canary, while imperfect, is a legitimate signal that the AI sector is now priced for perfection and any stumble triggers a re-rating.

**Why Underweight, not Sell:**
The bear won the debate on risk management, but the bull's fundamental facts prevent a full Sell. HBM *is* structurally different from commodity DRAM in important ways — co-engineering, qualification moats, and explosive demand growth create a floor that didn't exist in prior cycles. The earnings catalyst ahead (next few weeks) could deliver another blowout quarter, and the forward P/E — even on compressed margins — provides a valuation backstop. A Sell would require conviction that the cycle turns *imminently*, and the data doesn't support that timing with precision. But adding new money or maintaining a full position at these technical extremes and at this point in the memory cycle is reckless. Trim, take profits, and redeploy on a material pullback.
