## 2. Rail Industry – M&A & Competitive Landscape

### UP-NS Merger: Paused by STB
The biggest rail story this week was the **Surface Transportation Board (STB) conditionally accepting the Union Pacific–Norfolk Southern revised merger application but immediately freezing the review** and requesting additional data. Key fallout:
- **Union Pacific (UNP)** fell **-3.5%** and **Norfolk Southern (NSC)** dropped **-5%** on the news.
- **BNSF Railway** (owned by Berkshire Hathaway/BK) **remains firmly opposed**, stating the merger would "concentrate too much control."
- **Six state Attorneys General** urged the STB to reject the application.
- UBS commented on the STB's move to put the application on hold, noting the regulator is scrutinizing competitive impacts.

### Why This Matters for CP.TO:
- CPKC and BNSF have been **key opponents** of the UP-NS merger, arguing it would harm competition and create an effective duopoly in Western US rail.
- The STB's hesitation and demand for more data is a **positive signal for CP.TO** – it suggests the merger faces a difficult and lengthy regulatory path.
- If UP-NS fails or is significantly delayed, CPKC's unique **tri-national single-line network** (Canada-US-Mexico) retains its competitive moat.
- However, if UP-NS eventually succeeds, it would create a transcontinental competitor that could challenge CPKC's franchise.

### Broader Rail Sector Sentiment – Positive
- **CSX (CSX)** is up **+26% YTD**; unusual large institutional options activity suggests belief the rally has room to run (Barchart, May 28).
- **Canadian National Railway (CNI/CNR)** is up **+6% since last earnings** and named among *Best Transportation Stocks to Buy in 2026* by Motley Fool.
- **Trucking and logistics stocks** (RXO, Saia, Werner, Old Dominion, XPO, ArcBest, Knight-Swift) all rallied sharply on lower oil prices, which directly benefits railroads too.

## 3. Oil Prices & Fuel Cost Dynamics – Critical for CP.TO

Oil prices experienced **extreme volatility** this week driven by **US-Iran conflict dynamics**:

| Date | Event | WTI Move |
|------|-------|----------|
| May 27 (Wed) | Reports of US-Iran peace deal nearing | WTI **plunged -5.6%** to month low |
| May 28 (Thu) | Iran retaliatory strikes on US | Oil **rebounded** sharply |
| May 29 (Fri) | Reports of ceasefire extension; Trump sets "final determination" demands | WTI fell to **six-week low of ~$92.94** (down 4.7% in one session) |

**Key context:**
- **WTI crude closed at a six-week low of ~$92.94** on Friday on reports of a US-Iran ceasefire extension.
- Markets are pricing in a **60-day ceasefire extension** that could reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**.
- However, **Exxon warned** that physical crude inventories are near record lows and prices could spike to **$150–$160/barrel** once stockpiles are exhausted.
- **Scott Bessent (Treasury)** sent stark messages on oil prices and the economy.
- **US crude inventories** likely fell by 1.4 million barrels in the week ending May 22 (Macquarie).

### Why This Matters for CP.TO:
- **Lower oil prices = lower fuel costs** for railroads. Fuel is typically 15–25% of a railroad's operating expenses. A decline from elevated levels to ~$93/bbl provides meaningful **margin relief**.
- However, the situation is **highly binary**: a breakdown in Iran talks could send oil back above $100+, squeezing margins.
- CPKC has a **fuel surcharge mechanism** that partially offsets fuel cost increases, but there is a lag.

## 4. Canadian & Cross-Border Trade Developments

### CN's Alberta Energy Export Project
- **Canadian National Railway** announced a partnership with Keyera and AltaGas to develop the **Alberta Corridor Export Rail Terminal Project**, expanding export capacity for propane and butane.
- This highlights the strategic importance of **rail as a conduit for Canadian energy exports** – a theme that directly benefits CPKC given its extensive Canadian network and cross-border reach.

### Alberta Pipeline News
- An Alberta oil pipeline to the BC coast could cost the sector **more than C$100 billion**, Imperial Oil said. This underscores the **continued reliance on rail** for Canadian crude and product movement.

### Canadian Political Environment

### Broader US Dollar & Geopolitical Trends

- **Gold** hit record highs above $5,500/oz earlier in 2026; UBS just reset its gold price targets lower due to shifting rate dynamics.
