**Recommendation**: Underweight

**Rationale**: This was a genuinely close debate, with both sides landing heavy blows. 

The bull's strongest arguments are real: the semiconductor cycle *is* turning—Micron blew out, Infineon is +77% YTD, STMicro targets got raised 69%—and XFAB's revenue has held stable at ~$870M through the downturn, which tells you the company isn't losing customers. The Golden Cross and MACD surge signal a legitimate regime change after 18 months in a death cross, and the RSI cooling from 88 to 65 is exactly the pressure release you want after an initial breakout. The €635M in construction-in-progress is a genuine future revenue driver, not a boondoggle.

But the bear landed the more damaging blows on the balance sheet and valuation. A current ratio of 0.91 and negative working capital are not minor footnotes—they mean the company literally cannot cover its short-term obligations with current assets. The $292M in short-term debt against $192M in cash creates a funding gap that must be rolled over in a rising-rate environment. OCF annualized at $68M (from Q1 2026) is declining, not recovering. And critically, the operating margin collapse to 1.15% is not just "front-loaded depreciation"—SG&A has grown 37% while revenue is flat, suggesting genuine structural cost creep.

The killer: the stock has already rallied 150% from its $4.00 low. At €10.55 and an 81x P/E, the market has already priced in a recovery that hasn't appeared on the income statement. The bear's argument that there is no margin of safety here is persuasive. If the recovery takes another 6-12 months—or if oil above $100 further damages auto demand—the stock could easily retrace to the 50-SMA near €6.41, a ~40% drop.

That said, I cannot recommend a full Sell because the cyclical recovery thesis has genuine macro support, and the technical breakout—while overextended—is real. The bullish sector tailwinds (Micron, Infineon, ASML, Applied Materials all at highs) provide a floor that prevents me from going to Sell.

The right call is Underweight: trim into strength, don't add here, and wait for either price to come back to a better risk/reward level or fundamentals to confirm the recovery.
