### 1. 🚀 SNDK-Specific Developments

#### Memory Sector "Melt-Up" – SNDK Rallies 8% on May 26
The dominant story this week is the explosive rally in memory and semiconductor stocks. SNDK surged **~8%** on May 26 alone as part of a broader "memory melt-up" that also saw:
- **Micron Technology (MU)**: Soared **+16%** and crossed the **$1 trillion market cap** milestone for the first time after UBS analyst Timothy Arcuri tripled his price target.
- **Western Digital (WDC)**: Rose approximately **+10%** on the same session.

**Catalyst Behind the Rally:** UBS published one of the most aggressive sell-side revisions of the AI cycle, repricing the entire memory complex upward. Investor demand for memory chips tied to AI infrastructure is "soaring."

#### Russell 1000 Growth Index Inclusion
A preliminary list from **FTSE Russell** (released May 22) indicates that **SNDK will be added to the Russell 1000 Growth Index**. This index inclusion is a major passive inflow catalyst, as institutional funds tracking the index will need to accumulate SNDK shares. This news contributed to overnight/early-session strength.

#### SNDK: Top S&P 500 Performer Over the Past Year
Multiple sources (Motley Fool, Zacks) note that SNDK has been the **best-performing stock in the S&P 500 over the trailing 12 months**. Analysts are reportedly projecting the stock could **double from current levels**, driven by continued AI-driven memory demand.

#### ETF Tailwind: "DRAM" – Fastest-Growing ETF Ever
A memory-focused ETF (ticker: DRAM) has become the fastest-growing ETF in history amid the AI infrastructure buildout. This creates a positive feedback loop for names like SNDK within the semiconductor/memory ecosystem.

- **S&P 500** and **Nasdaq** pushed higher on the tech-led rally.

#### Sector Rotation
- Tech/memory/storage leading.
- Healthcare lagging (dragged lower on May 26).
- Metals and mining stocks showing strength on peace hopes.
- Consumer staples (Walmart) under strain.
