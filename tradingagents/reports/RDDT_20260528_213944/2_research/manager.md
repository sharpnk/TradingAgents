**Rationale**: This was a fiercely contested debate between two well-prepared analysts, and both sides landed heavy blows. Let me walk through how I weighed it.

The bull's strongest card is undeniable: Reddit just posted 69% YoY revenue growth, 28.6% net profit margins, and a staggering 46.9% free cash flow margin — all accelerating, not decelerating. The forward P/E of ~19x on that kind of growth is genuinely cheap, and the $2.75 billion net cash position provides a real downside buffer. The bull also correctly notes that the death cross is a lagging indicator that has already captured the 41% YTD decline; the more relevant signal is that the 50 SMA is rising and price is above it. The historical argument that Meta has never killed an innovator — Snapchat, TikTok, Clubhouse (which died of its own faddishness, not Meta) — also carries weight.

The bear, however, scored meaningful points on three fronts. First, the technical picture is objectively ugly: MACD negative with an expanding histogram, distribution-style volume on the May 20 selloff, and the 200 SMA sitting $42 above current price. These are not trivial and suggest the stock could easily grind lower before it recovers. Second, the Q1 sequential revenue decline of -8.6% is double the prior year's -3.8% — even if seasonal, the magnitude is worth watching. Third, stock-based compensation at 10.3% of revenue represents real ongoing dilution that tempers the headline margin story.

On the bear's other arguments, I found the bull's rebuttals more convincing. The Meta threat, while real, has rarely proven existential for established platforms. The macro headwind argument cuts both ways: performance advertising platforms often gain share in downturns as advertisers shift from brand to direct response. And the bear's GAAP EPS math that deducts SBC from earnings, while technically correct, is not how growth companies are typically valued by the market.

The decisive factor for me was the asymmetry: the bear's own realistic downside case points to ~$138 (about 10% downside), while the bull's realistic upside points to $188+ (about 22% upside). Combined with a fortress balance sheet and genuinely exceptional operating momentum, that skews the risk-reward in favor of constructive exposure — but not with the full conviction of a Buy rating, given the legitimate technical and competitive risks that could take months to resolve. Hence Overweight: gradually build the position, don't chase, and stay disciplined on stops.
