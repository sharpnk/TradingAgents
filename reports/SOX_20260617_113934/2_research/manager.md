The tiebreaker is the *sequence and character* of the price action. The Bear's strongest claim — that the +8% bounce was a dead cat — is not supported by the actual timeline. The volatility pattern (worst day → best day → rotation day) looks more like a violent shakeout within an uptrend than a topping formation. Meanwhile, sentiment at 4.0/10 with heavy retail bearishness is exactly the kind of wall of worry that bull markets climb. The Bear's case is well-argued but relies heavily on a generic valuation warning and a one-day rotation; the Bull's case is supported by the tape itself.

That said, the risks the Bear identifies are real and non-trivial. This is not a session for maximum bullishness. It is a session for constructive, measured exposure — adding on weakness, not chasing.
