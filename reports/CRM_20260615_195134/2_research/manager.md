The bull’s core thesis — that a world-class, cash-generating enterprise software franchise is temporarily mispriced — carried the argument because the valuation is simply too cheap to ignore. Even stripping out the buyback effect, organic operating income grew 16.5%, and the FCF coverage of interest is 8x+. The bear’s expected-value scenario work was thoughtful but leaned too heavily on multiple-compression assumptions that already appear priced in. The bull won the debate not on hope but on the weight of cash-flow evidence. That said, the bear’s technical warnings are real enough to warrant disciplined entry, not reckless aggression — hence Buy rather than an unconditional Strong Buy.
