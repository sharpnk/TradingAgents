## 1. Executive Summary

AMD remains a high-momentum AI semiconductor leader, having surged **+114% year-to-date** and **+290% over the past year.** However, on the analysis date (June 16), the stock pulled back sharply by **-7.3% to $507.29**, part of a broader chip-sector selloff that dragged the Nasdaq down -1.89%. The macro backdrop features a rotation away from tech growth stocks into industrial/financial names, the Dow hitting a record on an Iran oil deal, and growing caution around Fed rate hike signals. Despite the near-term weakness, AMD continues to ink strategic AI data-center partnerships (most notably a 30 MW deal with Rackspace Technology) and academic collaborations, reinforcing its long-term competitive position against Nvidia and other rivals.

2. **The Rackspace deal is a positive fundamental signal.** It demonstrates AMD's success in winning enterprise AI data-center customers, particularly in the regulated industry vertical. This supports the thesis that AMD is gaining share in AI compute, not just riding the sector wave.

3. **Watch the $490-$500 support zone.** Given the 114% YTD run, a deeper correction toward the 50-day moving average is plausible. A bounce off support with increasing volume would be the re-entry signal.

4. **Monitor Qualcomm's AI strategy.** If Qualcomm announces a major AI chip acquisition or detailed data-center roadmap, it could pressure AMD's long-term growth narrative.

5. **Macro catalyst watch:** Any dovish commentary from the Fed (Warsh or otherwise) or a Middle East peace deal closing could reverse the rotation and reignite the AI trade.
