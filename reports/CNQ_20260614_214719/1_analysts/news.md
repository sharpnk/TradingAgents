## 1. 🚨 THE DOMINANT MACRO EVENT: US-Iran Peace Deal

The single most consequential event of the past week is the **US-Iran peace deal**, declared "complete" by President Trump. This marks a dramatic de-escalation after months of conflict that had disrupted shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. The market reaction has been swift and powerful:

- **Oil prices plunged ~5.7%** in a single week, with Brent crude sliding sharply.
- **Stock markets surged** on the geopolitical risk relief (Dow futures, S&P 500, Nasdaq all rose).
- **Treasuries rallied** as traders trimmed Fed rate-hike bets following the oil price decline.
- Analysts now see **Brent at ~$80/barrel by year-end**, a significant downgrade from prior estimates that had baked in substantial Iran/Hormuz risk premium.
- The **oil risk premium has been fully removed** from crude prices, directly impacting the valuation and near-term outlook for all E&P companies, including CNQ.

> **Key Implication for CNQ:** As an oil sands and heavy crude producer, CNQ's revenues are highly correlated with the trajectory of crude prices. The peace deal is a **net bearish headwind** for near-term oil prices, though lower oil could also ease input costs and support global economic growth.
