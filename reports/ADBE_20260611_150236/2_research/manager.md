**Recommendation**: Buy

**Rationale**: After an exhaustive debate spanning multiple rounds, the bull case prevails on the weight of verifiable fundamental evidence. The bull demonstrated that Adobe's core business metrics are intact and in many cases strengthening: revenue accelerating to 12% YoY in Q1 FY2026, gross margins at a record 89.6%, free cash flow of $10.3B (up 19% in the latest quarter), and guidance raised by $550M+ explicitly citing AI adoption. The bear's strongest arguments—the technical breakdown and leadership vacuum—are real but ultimately transient. A 44% drawdown on a company that has doubled its revenue and tripled its FCF since 2019 represents a severe valuation dislocation, not structural decay. At 8.26x forward earnings, an 11.7% FCF yield, and a 12% annual buyback tailwind, the market is pricing in a worst-case scenario that the data does not support. The bear failed to identify a single deteriorating fundamental that withstands scrutiny: working capital deterioration is driven by growing deferred revenue (a bullish signal), the buyback gap is modest and easily funded by a $6.89B cash pile, and Digital Experience "deceleration" to 10.5% growth is a single-quarter data point in a segment still growing double digits. The leadership departures, while optically concerning, are a planned CEO succession after 20 years and a CFO poaching—not evidence of internal rot at a company generating $10B in annual cash flow. The risk/reward is compellingly asymmetric: ~50-100% upside to normalized multiples (15-20x) versus ~27% downside in a severe deterioration scenario. This is the most attractive large-cap software value opportunity in the current market.

**Strategic Actions**: **Position Sizing**: Initiate a position at 50% of the intended full allocation. This acknowledges the legitimate near-term risks (leadership uncertainty, bearish technical structure, potential for further short-term downside) while capturing the asymmetric upside.

**Entry Strategy**: 
- Place the initial tranche at current levels (~$218-225 range)
- Set a second buy order at $200-205 (the 2020 support zone, ~8-10% below current levels) to add the remaining 50% allocation. This level coincides with an FCF yield of ~13% and would represent extreme undervaluation
- If the stock reverses and breaks above the 50-day SMA (~$246) on volume, add the second tranche on the breakout to confirm a technical reversal

**Risk Management**:
- Hard stop-loss: $163 (27% below current). This corresponds to 8x FCF on a severely deteriorated $8B FCF scenario. If the business genuinely breaks, this is where to exit
- Soft alert: If two consecutive quarters show revenue growth below 8% or FCF growth below 5%, reassess the thesis
- Monitor the CEO succession timeline closely: a credible external hire should be a catalyst; a prolonged vacancy beyond Q3 2026 or an internal placeholder appointment would be a warning flag
- Watch for Digital Experience segment dipping below 8% growth—this would signal the AI monetization thesis is not converting

**Time Horizon**: 12-24 months. The thesis is that sentiment normalizes and the multiple expands to 15-20x as leadership is resolved and AI-driven revenue growth becomes undeniable. A new CEO announcement (expected within 1-2 quarters) is the nearest catalyst.
