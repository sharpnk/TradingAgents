The mechanical rebalancing has already been front‑run over the 10‑day window, and history suggests these events often become “sell the news” once the forced buying subsides. Post‑inclusion, RKLB will be left without a near‑term catalyst in a hostile macro environment.

3. **Valuation and Fundamentals Are Stretched**  
   At 98x trailing revenue, RKLB prices in near‑flawless execution that is far from guaranteed. The path to breakeven requires revenue to more than double (to ~$1.5B) while R&D and SG&A stay flat—a tall order when Neutron development costs are almost certain to rise. The company still burns $322M in free cash flow annually, and its $1.38B cash pile was built through massive dilution (33% share count increase in three years). The 12.6x return cited by the bull is backward‑looking; forward‑looking investors should weigh the real risk of further dilution if growth stalls.

4. **Duopoly Thesis Over‑promises**  
   Electron’s small‑lift niche cannot justify a $67B market cap, and the entire bull case hinges on Neutron—an un‑flown rocket facing a medium‑lift market that is about to become crowded. The defense tailwind is real, but government contracts are budget‑sensitive and do not insulate a stock from a multiple compression triggered by a hawkish Fed and rising rates.

5. **Extremely Poor Risk/Reward**  
   The consensus analyst target of $116.77 offers only 9% upside, while the technical downside to the 50‑SMA break is -17% ($89) and to the 200‑SMA is -32% ($73). With a daily ATR of $10.55, any reasonable stop‑loss would need to be $15–20 wide, risking 15% to make 9%. That is a sub‑1:1 reward‑to‑risk ratio, unacceptable for disciplined portfolio management.

### Decision: Underweight

The evidence does not support a full “Sell” rating—Rocket Lab is a viable company with a credible long‑term vision—but the current price is inflated by a temporary catalyst and extreme momentum that are now eroding. The bear’s call for a deeper correction to a realistic valuation zone ($73–$89) is well‑supported. Therefore, I recommend a cautious, underweight stance.
