- The WSJ reported "carnage in chip stocks" driven by "new concerns that trillions of dollars invested in AI technology won't yield expected blockbuster returns."
- Reuters noted "rumbling from corporate execs about AI token costs" causing companies to "think more mindfully about how they spend their AI budgets."

1. **Macro headwind is severe and dominant.** The blowout jobs report has reshaped the macro narrative toward "higher for longer" or even "hike again" for rates. BTC-USD historically trades as a high-beta risk asset in such environments.

2. **$60,000 is now resistance (former support).** After dipping below $60,000 and recovering slightly, this level will be watched as a battleground. A failure to reclaim it decisively opens the door to the next major support zone around **$50,000-$52,000** (pre-election levels).

4. **Weekend risk is elevated.** Peter Brandt's "Friday closing loss rule" and warnings of a "Black Monday" suggest traders should be cautious about holding long positions over the weekend.

5. **Correlation with AI/tech stocks is high.** The Nasdaq and BTC are moving in lockstep. A stabilization in AI stocks (potential catalyst: Apple WWDC announcements) could help BTC, but the rate-hike overhang is powerful.

6. **Geopolitical risk (Iran War) is adding to commodity inflation**, which further complicates the Fed's path and keeps pressure on risk assets.
