### 2. Investment Thesis Summary

**Bull Case**  
- **Financial fortitude:** $2.75B trailing revenue (+63% YoY), $486M TTM FCF, only $36.8M total debt against $1.5B unrestricted cash.  
- **Earnings normalization post‑IPO:** Stripping one‑time SBC, the business is profitable today; a return to H2’25 earnings run rate makes the forward P/E of ~37x look compelling relative to growth.  
- **Regulatory catalyst:** The CLARITY Act is a near‑term binary event that could re‑rate the stock by 50‑100% if passed, while failure leaves the business still growing globally.  
- **Asymmetric risk/reward:** At ~$79, probability‑weighted expected return is strongly positive (bull’s estimate +56%; even a more conservative blend remains attractive).

**Bear Case (Residual Risks)**  
- **Competitive erosion:** If the Visa/Mastercard/Stripe consortium materializes and Coinbase participates, USDC’s distribution advantage could shrink rapidly.  
- **Macro gloom:** Bitcoin near two‑year lows suppresses Crypto‑related revenue; a prolonged bear market would delay the earnings recovery.  
- **Technical downtrend:** No confirmed bottom; the stock could test $50 or lower before stabilizing.

**Why OVERWEIGHT rather than full BUY?**  
The competitive risk, while uncertain, is real and evolving. We want to build exposure gradually, respecting the still‑bearish price trend, rather than committing all capital immediately. This stance allows us to participate in the upside of a potential resolution (CLARITY Act, bounce from oversold levels) while limiting short‑term drawdown.
