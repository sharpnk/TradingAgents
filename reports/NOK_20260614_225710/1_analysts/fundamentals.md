## 11. Final Assessment & Actionable Insights

**Bull Case:** Nokia's fundamentals are showing signs of a genuine turnaround — revenue stabilized in FY2025, Q1 2026 showed improving profitability, the balance sheet is net cash positive, FCF generation is strong at $1.6B+ TTM, and the company continues to invest heavily in 5G/6G leadership. The Forward P/E of 30.4x, while elevated, could prove justified if Nokia achieves the projected EPS of $0.49 (a 3x improvement over TTM EPS of $0.16).

**Bear Case:** The stock has already rallied **+336%** from its $4 low, and the current TTM P/E of **92.5x** is pricing in perfection. Margins remain thin, revenue is still well below 2021 peaks, ROE is only 3.7%, and the forward estimates may prove optimistic if telecom capex cycles soften. The PEG ratio of 1.43 suggests growth expectations are already priced in.

**Key Risk/Reward:** At ~$82.6B market cap with TTM net income of ~$774M and FCF of ~$1.6B, the stock trades at a significant premium to its earnings power. The market is betting on a dramatic earnings recovery. For the current P/E to compress to a more reasonable 30x (forward P/E), Nokia would need to deliver on the ~$0.49 EPS estimates — a tripling of current earnings power.

**Verdict:** NOK exhibits a **mixed fundamental picture**: very strong balance sheet and cash flows, but extremely rich valuation after a massive rally. The turnaround story is plausible but already heavily priced in.
