## 1. RY-Specific Developments

### 1.1 Analyst Upgrade & Price Target Raise
On **June 1, 2026**, Scotiabank raised its price target on **RY** to **C$275** from **C$252**, maintaining an **Outperform** rating. Barclays also issued a rating update on the stock. This bullish analyst sentiment signals confidence in RY's earnings trajectory and valuation. RY has also been featured in multiple "undervalued stocks" lists (Simply Wall St.), suggesting it may be trading below intrinsic value relative to its fundamentals.

### 1.2 Normal-Course Issuer Bid (Share Buyback)
**RY announced a Normal-Course Issuer Bid** to repurchase up to **45 million shares**. This is a strong signal of management's confidence in the bank's financial health, capital adequacy, and future prospects. Share buybacks tend to support earnings per share and provide a floor under the stock price.

### 1.3 Dividend Strength Highlighted
Zacks featured **RY** as a potential **high-growth dividend stock**, underscoring its reputation as a reliable dividend payer with growth potential — an attractive feature in a volatile market environment.

### 1.4 Canadian Housing & Consumer Spending Context
The **WSJ Financial Services Roundup** referenced **Canadian cardholder spending** and **Canadian home prices** — both directly relevant to RY's domestic operations. As Canada's largest bank by market capitalization, RY has significant exposure to the Canadian mortgage market and consumer credit. Trends in these areas will influence RY's net interest income and loan loss provisions.

## 2. Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Environment

### 2.1 Iran / Middle East Crisis (HIGH IMPACT)
The geopolitical landscape remains extremely volatile:
- **Strait of Hormuz** has been a flashpoint — initially a US-Iran deal to reopen the waterway pushed oil to a 3-month low, but negotiations have **stalled over Lebanon**.
- **Iran says Hormuz closed again** and talks with the US were delayed.
- Tanker traffic thinning again as shipowners err on the side of safety.
- Brent crude trading **above $80/barrel** amid the uncertainty.

**Implication for RY:** Canada is a major oil-producing nation. Higher/volatile oil prices are a double-edged sword — they boost the Canadian economy (supporting RY's domestic loan book and energy sector exposure) but also fuel inflation and consumer uncertainty.

### 2.2 Federal Reserve Transition – Kevin Warsh
- **Kevin Warsh is taking over as Fed Chair**, and his first meeting could significantly impact markets.
- The **Treasury market is signaling rates need to be higher** — two-year yields hit a 15-month high.
- The Fed faces a challenge: **inflation is stickier** than desired (the "4% becoming the new 2%" risk).

**Implication for RY:** Higher interest rates generally benefit bank net interest margins (NIM), which is positive for RY's earnings. However, if rates rise too aggressively, it could slow economic growth and increase credit risk.

### 2.3 Market Conditions
- **US market down ~2.5–4.1% over the past week**, though up ~21–23% over the past year.
- Earnings are expected to grow ~17% annually.
- Barron's warns of **stocks flirting with a dangerous valuation trap**.
- Major IPOs from OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX — representing ~$4 trillion in potential market cap — could drain liquidity from existing equities.

### 2.4 Russia-Ukraine Conflict
- Russia struck a historic UNESCO-protected cathedral in Kyiv after a **Trump-Putin phone call**.
- The conflict continues to escalate, adding to global uncertainty and energy price pressures.

### 2.5 UK Political Instability
- Andy Burnham won a decisive victory, giving him a path to challenge UK PM Starmer.
- Political instability in a major economy adds to global uncertainty.

### 2.6 Consumer Health Concerns
- **Shoe prices continue to rise**, job concerns are mounting, and the consumer is described as "shaky."
- This suggests potential headwinds for consumer credit quality and spending.
