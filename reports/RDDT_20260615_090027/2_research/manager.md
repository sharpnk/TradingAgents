**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: After an exhaustive, multi-round debate, the bull carries the argument on the weight of fundamentals — but the bear's technical and macro cautions are too substantive to dismiss entirely. This is not a dead cat; it's a structurally improved business caught in a tug-of-war between lagging technicals and leading fundamentals.

**What the bull got right:** The operational transformation is undeniable. Reddit swung from a $484M loss to a $530M profit, generated $684M in free cash flow in FY2025 (31.1% margin), and posted a 46.9% FCF margin in Q1 2026 — a quarter that was *negative* FCF the prior year. The balance sheet is pristine ($2.77B cash, $21M debt). SBC declined from 61.6% of revenue to 15.6% and, while plateauing, continues to shrink as a percentage of revenue as the top line grows. The forward P/E of ~19.5x with a PEG of 1.16 is reasonable for a company with 69% revenue growth and 91.5% gross margins. The data licensing moat — anchored by the Google deal and the Anthropic legal precedent — is early but real, providing a non-advertising revenue stream that competitors cannot easily replicate. The 50-day SMA is rising, and the stock is above it — that is the medium-term trend that matters most.

**What the bear got right:** The short-term technical picture is genuinely bearish. A fresh MACD crossover, RSI below 50, price below the 10-day EMA and Bollinger mid, and a widening death cross all demand respect. The June 12 selloff on 5.76 million shares (well above average) is distribution-level volume. SBC at $343M is plateauing, not disappearing, and adjusted earnings net of SBC are far lower than GAAP suggests. The sequential revenue decline from Q4 to Q1 is a yellow flag for deceleration risk. Macro headwinds — sticky inflation, consumer anxiety, and Barron's "valuation trap" warning — are real for a high-beta (1.91) ad-dependent name. Retail sentiment on Reddit's own platform is conspicuously absent, and the Roku M&A comp is thin.

**Why the bull prevails:** The bear's case is anchored in backward-looking technicals and forward-looking macro fears. The bull's case is anchored in *current, demonstrated operating results* that have structurally improved. Companies that go from cash-incineration to 30%+ FCF margins do not typically stay at 19.5x forward earnings for long. The death cross reflects the $270→$120 crash, not the $120→$184 recovery. The rising 50-day SMA is the live trend. The balance sheet provides a cushion against macro pain that most high-growth peers lack. The 28% YTD decline is a valuation compression that has made a premium asset affordable — not a verdict on the business.

The prudent stance is to recognize the bear's tactical warnings (don't go all-in at once; respect the $158.50 level as a line in the sand) while positioning for the bull's strategic thesis to play out over the next 6–12 months.
