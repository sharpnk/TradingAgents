## 2. Global Macroeconomic Context

### ✅ U.S.-Iran Peace Deal Signed
The **U.S. and Iran signed a peace deal** this past week, which helped drive a Nasdaq and Russell 2000 rebound. This de-escalation removes a major geopolitical risk and is broadly positive for industrial and aerospace stocks like RBC, which had been weighed down by supply chain concerns related to the conflict.

### 🔶 Fed Transition — Kevin Warsh Incoming
Kevin Warsh is taking over the Fed. Barrons notes his first meeting "could slam the stock market." The market is pricing in uncertainty around the pace and direction of monetary policy under new leadership. This is a medium-term risk.

### 🔶 Inflation Still Sticky
The Fed "can't let 4% become the new 2% inflation target," per Barrons. Sticky inflation could keep interest rates higher for longer, which would pressure growth-oriented industrial valuations. However, RBC's strong operational performance may offset this headwind.

### 🔶 Valuation Trap Warning
Barrons warns that "stocks are flirting with a dangerous valuation trap." This reinforces Simply Wall St.'s caution about RBC's rich P/E multiple and is a key risk factor for new entries.

### ✅ Consumer & Job Concerns — Mixed Signals
Shoe prices continue to rise, and there are job concerns and a shaky consumer backdrop. However, RBC is an industrial/B2B company less directly exposed to consumer discretionary weakness than other sectors.
