**Investment Thesis**: The bull wins the debate on the weight of forward-looking, verifiable fundamentals. Revenue re-accelerated to 22.1% YoY in Q1 2026, management raised the AI revenue target to a specific $1.5B — a number they are now accountable to deliver — and TTM free cash flow of $5.11B produces a 4.76% FCF yield, top-decile for a SaaS company growing at this rate. The PEG ratio of 0.99, below 1.0 for the first time in years, represents a rare mispricing for a best-in-class enterprise software platform. Management deployed $2.23B in buybacks in a single quarter — more than all of FY2025 combined — retiring ~0.7% of the float. That is the loudest conviction signal a management team can send, and it dwarfs backward-looking technical indicators.

The bear correctly identifies risks that prevent a full Buy rating: gross margins compressed 400bp YoY (78.9% to 75.1%), goodwill ballooned from $1.27B to $4.54B in one quarter on acquisition activity that introduces integration risk, and the stock underperformed the Nasdaq by over 800bp in the week following the IBM partnership announcement — a tepid reception that warrants caution. However, the bear's case relies predominantly on backward-looking technical indicators (death cross, 200-day SMA, MACD) that still incorporate stale pre-split price data, and on macro/sentiment arguments that are cyclical rather than structural. The bear did not demonstrate that the business itself is deteriorating; revenue is accelerating, not decelerating.

The volume profile supports the bull's accumulation thesis: daily volume on the pullback declined from 68.8M (June 1 peak) to 41.4M to 35.8M to 26M — consistent with selling exhaustion, not accelerating distribution. The IBM partnership is a genuinely strategic alliance targeting the single biggest barrier to enterprise AI adoption (fragmented legacy data), and judging a multi-year partnership on a one-week price move is a category error. Catalysts over the next 60 days — Q2 earnings (July), first IBM joint customer announcements, and potential sector rotation once SpaceX IPO liquidity normalizes — are not yet fully priced and provide near-term upside drivers. The thesis is anchored in management's demonstrated capital allocation discipline, accelerating revenue trajectory, and a valuation that prices in stagnation rather than the AI-driven growth that is already materializing.
