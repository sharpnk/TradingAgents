## 1. Global Macroeconomic Landscape

### 1.1 Fed Leadership Shake-Up: Kevin Warsh Takes the Helm
The biggest macro story this week is **Kevin Warsh's debut as Federal Reserve Chairman**. His first press conference sent shockwaves through bond markets, with traders aggressively pricing in interest-rate hikes as soon as next month. Warsh made clear the Fed "won't tolerate high inflation," signaling a definitive break from the previous dovish posture. This has major implications for BMO and the broader banking sector:
- **Wider net interest margins** if rate hikes materialize — a positive for bank profitability.
- **Pressure on bond prices**, potential equity market volatility.
- A strengthening USD (contributing to CAD weakness — see below).

### 1.2 Inflation: The 4% Threshold Battle
A key Barron's article highlights the Fed's challenge of preventing inflation from settling above 2%. With inflation still elevated, the new Fed chair's hawkish rhetoric may lead to tighter monetary conditions sooner than markets anticipated.

## 2. Canada-Specific Macroeconomic Environment

### 2.1 Canadian Dollar Hits Multi-Year Low
The **CAD fell to 70.7 US cents** (above $1.413/USD), its lowest level in some time. BMO attributes this to:
- **USMCA trade review concerns** (bilateral trade uncertainty).
- **USD strength** from the more hawkish Fed perception under Warsh.
- **Implication:** A weaker CAD benefits BMO's US-dollar-denominated earnings when translated back to CAD, and may support export-oriented sectors in the bank's lending book.

### 2.2 OSFI Cuts Domestic Stability Buffer (DSB)
Canada's bank regulator (**OSFI**) cut the **capital buffer** required for big banks. This is a **directly positive regulatory development for BMO**:
- Frees up capital that can be deployed for lending.
- Aligns with government efforts to kick-start the economy.
- Improves BMO's return on equity (ROE) potential.

### 2.3 Canadian Housing Market — Signs of a Bottom
BMO's Economics team published multiple notes this week:
- **"Beautiful lull"** — the floor under Canada's hardest-hit housing markets may be firming.
- **May home sales rebounded**, and the market is "holding balance."
- **Toronto housing** is "no longer detached from reality" after four years of correction.
- **Housing starts** remain solid at 261,000 annualized units in May.
- **Provincial variation** remains "stark."
- **Rentals** are taking a bigger slice of new supply.

### 2.4 Canadian Households — Resilient but Indebted
- Households hold **$8+ trillion in liquid financial assets** — a buffer supporting the economy.
- Debt-to-income ratio rose 0.9 ppt to **179%** — still elevated but manageable.
- **Population decline** continues ("fizzling out" per BMO), which has long-term implications for housing and economic growth.
