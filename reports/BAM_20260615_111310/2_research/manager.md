- The market has absorbed the announced catalysts (Boralex closing, Oaktree progress) and still marked the stock down 11% YTD—suggesting the balance sheet concerns are outweighing the growth narrative for now.

**Where the debate settled:**
The Bull won the argument on *business quality* and *long-term compounding potential*. The Bear won the argument on *near-term risk/reward* and *balance sheet sustainability*. Neither side landed a knockout blow on the other's core territory.

The Bull could not definitively prove that the cash flow deficit is benign—distributable earnings is an adjusted metric, not GAAP, and even it shows thin coverage. The Bear could not definitively prove the business is deteriorating—revenue, AUM, and fee-related earnings are all growing.

**Why Hold rather than Overweight or Underweight:**
This is the rare case where the evidence is genuinely balanced. A Hold acknowledges that the 4.26% yield is being paid, the business is compounding, and management is buying—so selling makes little sense. But the leverage trajectory, thin dividend coverage, and bearish medium-term technical structure argue against adding to the position until one of two things happens: either the price reclaims the 200-day SMA on volume (confirming a trend reversal), or the stock retests $45 and holds again (offering a better entry with a defined stop). Patience is warranted.
