## 2. Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Context

### 🟢 **Major Positive Catalyst: US-Iran Peace Deal**
The most significant macro event of the week:
- **President Trump announced a US-Iran peace deal** described as "complete"
- **Oil prices plunged** (crude fell ~5.7% for the week)
- **Stock markets surged** globally, with Dow Jones futures rising sharply
- **Treasuries rallied**, traders trimmed Fed rate hike bets
- Analysts project Brent oil at **$80 by year-end**

**Implication for TD:** Lower oil prices reduce energy sector credit risk, easing pressure on TD's Canadian loan portfolio. Falling bond yields also support fixed-income asset valuations.

### ⚠️ **Canada in Technical Recession**
Bloomberg reported that **Canada has dipped into a technical recession** (two consecutive quarters of contraction) for the first time since 2020. This is a **key headwind** for TD given its significant domestic exposure.

### 🟢 **Canadian Household Wealth Rises to $18.6 Trillion**
Despite the recession, Statistics Canada reported household wealth rose by **$243 billion (+1.3% QoQ)** in Q1 2026, driven by strength in stocks and real estate. This supports consumer health and deposit growth for TD.

### ⚠️ **Fed Inflation Concerns – "4% as the New 2%"**
The Fed is wrestling with inflation that refuses to fall below 4%, creating uncertainty about the pace of future rate cuts. Barrons raised the question of whether the market has prematurely priced in rate normalization. Higher-for-longer rates are a double-edged sword for banks: they boost net interest margins but risk dampening loan demand and increasing defaults.

### ⚠️ **Valuation Trap Warning**
Multiple sources (Barrons, others) warn that **stocks are flirting with a dangerous valuation trap** after the post-Iran deal rally. Investors should be cautious about chasing price momentum without fundamental support.

### Consumer & Job Market Concerns
Signals of a shaky consumer: rising shoe prices (inflation persistence), job concerns, and potentially slower retail sales ahead. This could impact TD's consumer loan and credit card portfolios.

## 6. Trading & Investment Implications

**Bull Case:**
TD enters a period of strong momentum: record earnings, rising dividends, multiple analyst upgrades, digital leadership, and a favorable macro catalyst (US-Iran peace deal driving lower oil, lower rates, and higher equity markets). Canadian household wealth is rising, supporting wealth management growth.

**Bear Case:**
Canada's technical recession poses a real risk to domestic loan growth and credit quality. Stubborn inflation (4%) could keep rates higher for longer than expected. Broad market valuations are elevated, increasing downside risk.

**Overall Assessment:**
The balance of evidence leans **BULLISH** on TD. The record Q2 results, dividend hike, and broad-based analyst upgrades provide strong fundamental support. The US-Iran peace deal adds a powerful macro tailwind. The Canadian recession is a concern, but rising household wealth and TD's diversified business model (including its US retail operations) help mitigate this risk.

**Next Key Catalysts to Watch:**
- Bank of Canada interest rate decision (impact on NIM)
- Q3 FY2026 earnings (expected August/September)
- Further analyst price target revisions
- US economic data (inflation, employment) for Fed policy direction
